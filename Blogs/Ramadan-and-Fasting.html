Ramadan and Fasting: Tips and Benefits

27 Mar, 2023 / by Al-Hanoof Al-Amri


What is Ramadan?

Ramadan is a month when Muslims fast from dawn (Fajr) to dusk (iftar or Maghrib), abstaining from food, drink, impure thoughts and activities. It is a time to concentrate on prayers and establish a closer relationship with Allah (SWT).

Ramadan has a way deeper meaning than this. Fasting makes one more grounded and appreciative of everything Allah (SWT) has given them, by allowing them to comprehend the agony and suffering endured by millions of people worldwide who live in famine and poverty, but still strive and work hard during the day. Why Fast?

Apart from the spiritual benefits of fasting, it also has several health benefits if done correctly. For example, fasting has been proven to help shift the body towards fat burning, eliminate toxins, and keep diabetes under control.

  • Fat Burning

    • Fasting has been proven to help shift the body towards fat burning. What happens is when you fast, your body uses the nutrients from the last meal to make energy. Once all the nutrients have been absorbed, the body shifts towards the stores (livers and muscles) and lastly, the preserved fats.



  • Remove Toxins

    • The body recycles and disassembles unneeded cells while fasting. As a result, toxins accumulated in fat and other areas are also eliminated. This process is associated with reducing inflammation; which is a root cause of so many chronic illnesses.



  • Keeping Diabetes at Bay

    • Ramadan has a major effect on diabetes control. When the body starts using fat reserves as energy, this will aid in controlling blood glucose, blood pressure and cholesterol.

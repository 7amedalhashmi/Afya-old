Importance of Hydration for Kidney Health

27 Mar, 2023 / by Reem Ali Alsharqi

Hydration plays a crucial role in maintaining kidney health. Our kidneys rely heavily on water to carry out their tasks, which will be mentioned below. In this blog, we will discuss the importance of hydration for kidney health, how dehydration affects the kidneys, and provide tips to drink more water.


Importance of Hydration for Kidney Health

Research shows that enough hydration can help prevent the formation of kidney stones, which can lead to serious health problems. Kidneys are two bean-shaped organs located on either side of the spine, directly below the ribcage. They are responsible for a number of crucial bodily processes, such as removing waste and extra fluid from the blood, controlling blood pressure, and generating hormones that help regulate the creation of red blood cells and bone health.

Body's Fluid Requirements

The daily fluid requirements for healthy people depend on several factors such as age, gender, and exercise. To identify how many liters you need daily, follow this simple equation:

35 x weight (kg) = [your daily fluid requirements]

How Dehydration Affects the Kidneys

When the body loses more water than it absorbs through sweating, urinating, or some other process, it becomes dehydrated. Many symptoms accompany dehydration, such as exhaustion, headaches, dry mouth, and decreased urine flow.

Dehydration can significantly impact the kidneys, as they work harder to maintain normal functions when the body is dehydrated. This can result in less blood passing through the kidneys, which in turn, less waste and extra fluid being filtered out of the blood. Eventually, this can cause the body to accumulate waste products, raising the risk of kidney damage and illness. For example, dehydration can make urine more concentrated, which can make it more likely for tiny, hard deposits of minerals and salts to adhere to one another and form kidney stones.

Short- and Long-Term Impact of Dehydration on Kidneys

Dehydration can have a serious effect on the health of the kidneys, both in the short-term and long-term.

Short-term dehydration can cause a reduction in urine production, which may indicate that the kidneys are not working properly. Additionally, it may result in an accumulation of waste products in the body, which can cause a number of symptoms like weariness, nausea, and dizziness.

Dehydration in the long-term can raise the risk of renal disease and damage, as the kidneys work much harder, trying to compensate and bring balance back. This can lead to kidney damage and even chronic kidney disease, which can be serious and even fatal.

Tips to Drink More Water

The CDC recommends the following tips to drink more water:

  • Drink water as you eat.
  • If you're eating out, choose water to save both money and calories.
  • Choose water over sweetened beverages.
  • Ensure that your children are drinking enough water.
  • Before, during, and after exercise, drink water.
  • Always have a water bottle with you.
  • Create phone reminders to consistently consume water.
  • Consume foods high in water, such as fruits and vegetables.
  • Consume other beverage options besides water such as low or no calorie beverages and nutrient-rich beverages.

Because of the hot climate in the UAE, the CDC encourages more water intake to stay hydrated.

Conclusion

Proper hydration is crucial for maintaining healthy kidneys and preventing kidney damage. Adequate hydration can help prevent kidney stone formation and maintain normal kidney functions. To stay hydrated and protect your kidneys, follow the tips mentioned above and ensure you're consuming enough water every day. Remember, taking care of your kidneys today can lead to a healthier tomorrow.

The Importance of Food Safety: Tips to Ensure Safe and Healthy Eating

26 Jun, 2023 / by Fatima Alhammadi and Khawla Alzubaidi

Food is a crucial aspect of human survival, providing energy and nourishment. It is essential to ensure that the food we consume is safe and free of any hazards that could negatively impact our health. Food safety refers to the necessary practices during the handling, processing, and distribution of food to prevent food-borne illnesses and injuries. Moreover, It helps to prevent risks of health-related conditions such as allergies and even death. Did you know that at least 600 million people worldwide are affected by food-borne illnesses every year due to consuming unsanitary food? To avoid such risks, it is essential to have knowledge about what could compromise the integrity and stability of food. Firstly, you must take simple measures that guarantee that you are preparing and consuming food in the right way, Such as hand-washing. It is an essential most effective way to prevent cross-contamination and to get rid of the harmful germs you are carrying around in your hands. Use these four easy methods to protect your family from food-borne illness: clean, separate, cook, and chill.

Here are some simple tips to guarantee the safe preparation and consumption of food:

Handwashing

Washing your hands is the most effective way to prevent cross-contamination and to get rid of harmful germs.

When should you wash your hands?
  • Before, during, and after preparing any food.
  • After handling uncooked meat, chicken, or other poultry, seafood, flour, or eggs.
  • Before and after using gloves to prevent germs from spreading to your food and your hands.
  • Before eating.
  • After touching garbage.
  • After wiping counters or cleaning other surfaces with chemicals.
  • After touching pets, pet food, or pet treats.
  • After coughing, sneezing, or blowing your nose.
How should you wash your hands properly?

To get the full benefit of hand-washing, follow these five steps:

  1. Wet your hands with clean running water (warm or cold) and apply soap.
  2. Lather your hands together, including the backs of your hands, between your fingers, and under your nails.
  3. Scrub your hands for at least 20 seconds.
  4. Rinse your hands well under clean running water.
  5. Dry your hands using a clean towel or air dry them.
Separation

To avoid food-borne illnesses, it's crucial to separate kitchen utensils and keep uncooked meat, poultry, seafood, and eggs apart from other goods in your fridge and shopping bags. Use a different chopping board for each food type and never put prepared, cooked meals on a dish that was recently used for raw meat, poultry, seafood, or eggs, unless it is thoroughly cleaned.

Cooking Safely

To eliminate potentially hazardous microorganisms, it's essential to cook food to the minimum inner temperature. A food thermometer is the most accurate way to determine the temperature. The following table shows the minimum inner temperature for different types of food:

Chilling

Storing food correctly is crucial, and since most food goes into our fridge and freezer, it's essential to know how to chill food properly.

  • Make sure the freezer is consistently -17 ¬∞C or below, and for the fridge 4.44 ¬∞C.
  • Two hours after cooking or purchasing, put beef, chicken, eggs, seafood, and perishable items in the fridge or freezer.
  • Food should not defrost at room temperature, but can be safely defrosted in the fridge, in cold water, or using the microwave.
  • Food should always be marinated in the fridge.
